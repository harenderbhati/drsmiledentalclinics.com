Dental Implants

A dental implant is a prosthesis that interfaces with the bone of the jaw or skull to support a dental prosthesis such as a crown, bridge, denture, or facial prosthesis or to act as an orthodontic anchor.

Cosmetic Dentistry

Cosmetic dentistry is a method of professional oral care that focuses on improving the appearance of your teeth.

Single Sitting Roots Canal

Single Sitting RCT is the standard treatment for removing an infection from a damaged or decayed tooth instead of removing it.

Dental pain Management

Patients may present to a medical practitioner with dental pain, dento-maxillofacial trauma and treatment-related complications. This happens frequently in emergency departments, but also in general practice.

Fixed & Removable Dentures

After wearing fixed dentures, you don't need to remove them before going to be or clean them. They look, function, and feel exactly like natural teeth.

Orthodontic Braces

Orthodontics is a dental specialty focused on aligning your bite and straightening your teeth. You might need to see an orthodontist if you have crooked, overlapped, twisted or gapped teeth.

Paediatric Dentistry

Pediatric dentistry is one of the seven recognized clinical specialties of dentistry. It includes all areas of adult dentistry that may be applied to children.

Laser Dentistry

Laser dentistry is the use of lasers to treat a number of different dental conditions. It became commercially used in clinical dental practice for procedures involving tooth tissue in 1989.

TMJ Disorders

The temporomandibular joint or TMJ acts like a sliding hinge, connecting your jawbone to your skull.

Full Mouth Rehabilitation

As the name implies, full mouth reconstruction refers to rebuilding and/or replacing all of the teeth in a patient's mouth.

Extraction/Impactions

The decision to extract a distal impacted tooth depends on the angle of the tooth and the extent of impaction

Digital Intraoral Scan

Intraoral scanners, or digital scanners in dentistry, are tools that give dental practitioners a digital alternative to the traditional method of taking an analog impression.

Restoractive Dentistry

Restorative dentistry focuses on repairing or replacing damaged or missing teeth.
